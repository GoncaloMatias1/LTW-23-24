The Washington Post

By Goncalo Matias, 15/2/2024

  • Former mayor of Caminha, Miguel Alves, and businesswoman Manuela Sousa were acquitted of malfeasance related to public contracting for communication consultancy services.
  • Court's Decision: Highlighted the lack of evidence to support the accusation by the Public Prosecutor (MP) against the defendants.
  • Miguel Alves faced accusations of violating public contracting rules by agreeing to communication consultancy services with Manuela Sousa without a public procurement process.
  • In January, during closing arguments, the MP sought conviction based on documentary evidence and experiential rules, but the court disagreed.
  • Post-verdict, Alves spoke on the personal and family impact of the accusations, describing the damage as natural but irreparable.
